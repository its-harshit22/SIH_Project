Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) Information

What is DBT?

Direct Benefit Transfer (DBT) is an initiative by the Government of India to directly transfer the benefits and subsidies of various welfare schemes to the beneficiaries' bank accounts. This helps to reduce leakages, delays, and ensures transparency in the process.

How DBT Works for Farmers

  • Farmers register on the platform with their Aadhaar number and bank account details.
  • When a claim is verified, compensation is directly transferred to the farmer's bank account.
  • This eliminates the need for intermediaries, ensuring that the full amount reaches the beneficiary.

Benefits of DBT

  • Ensures timely delivery of financial support to farmers.
  • Reduces corruption by eliminating middlemen.
  • Improves transparency in the distribution of subsidies and benefits.
  • Empowers farmers by providing them with direct access to government support.
